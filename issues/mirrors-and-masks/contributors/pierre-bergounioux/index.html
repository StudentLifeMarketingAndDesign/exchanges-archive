Pierre Bergounioux

Pierre Bergounioux was born in Brive-la-Gaillarde, in the department of CorrÃ¨ze, in 1949. He currently lives and teaches literature near Paris. He has written and published extensively. In his autobiographically-based novels he often returns to the same subjects to patiently explore his main concerns: life and the unrelenting work of time. He writes about memory, hope and the disruptions caused by modern technology and changing mindsets. Mr. Bergounioux wrote a thesis on Flaubert under the direction of Roland Barthes and was strongly influenced by the ways in which Faulkner reworked the novel. He won the Prix Roger Caillois for his body of work in 2009.
